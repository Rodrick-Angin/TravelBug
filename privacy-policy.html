TravelBug Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 19, 2026

TravelBug is designed to help you track travel history with privacy-first defaults. Most processing happens on your device. This policy explains what data the app can access, how it is used, and the choices you have.

1. Scope

This policy applies to the TravelBug iOS app and related website pages operated by TravelBug.

2. Data TravelBug Can Access

Depending on the features you use, TravelBug may access:

  • Travel data you create: flights, places, routes, notes, and app settings.
  • Apple Calendar data (optional): read-only access to event details needed to detect travel events, such as title, date/time, and location.
  • Google Calendar data (optional): read-only access to event details needed to detect travel events, such as title, date/time, location, and description.
  • Google account basic profile (optional): account email used to show signed-in status and manage Google Calendar access.
  • iCloud key-value storage data (optional): app data you choose to sync across your own devices.

TravelBug does not request write access to your calendars for import/sync features.

3. How Data Is Used

  • Detect and import travel-related events.
  • Build and maintain your travel timeline, maps, and statistics.
  • Sync your TravelBug data across your Apple devices if you enable iCloud Backup and Sync.
  • Optionally enrich flight metadata (for example, airline and airport details) when enrichment is enabled.

4. Where Data Is Stored

  • On-device by default: TravelBug stores your core travel records locally on your device.
  • iCloud (optional): if you enable Backup and Sync, selected TravelBug data is synced via your private iCloud account using Apple services.

5. Network Requests and Third-Party Services

TravelBug does not operate a separate TravelBug-hosted backend for your travel records. When network access is needed, requests are made from your device directly to the service you chose to use:

  • Google Sign-In and Google Calendar API (only if you connect Google Calendar).
  • Apple EventKit / Calendar (device calendar access, controlled by iOS permission settings).
  • Apple Maps / MapKit (map rendering and related map services).
  • Aviationstack API (optional flight enrichment if enabled in the app build).
  • Apple iCloud Key-Value Storage (only if you enable Backup and Sync).

Third-party services process data under their own terms and privacy policies.

6. Google API Services User Data

If you connect Google Calendar, TravelBug requests read-only calendar scope to identify travel events. TravelBug does not use Google user data for advertising.

TravelBug use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy , including the Limited Use requirements.

7. Data Sharing, Sale, and Advertising

  • TravelBug does not sell your personal data.
  • TravelBug does not use your calendar or travel data for third-party advertising.
  • TravelBug shares data only when required to provide features you explicitly enable (for example, Google Calendar import, iCloud sync, optional flight enrichment), or when you choose to share content yourself via iOS share features.

8. Retention

Your data remains in TravelBug until you delete it in the app, remove the app, or disable and clear synced storage in your Apple account settings. Third-party providers may retain data according to their own policies.

9. Your Controls

  • Grant or revoke Apple Calendar permission in iOS Settings.
  • Connect or disconnect your Google account in TravelBug and your Google account permissions page.
  • Enable or disable iCloud Backup and Sync in TravelBug settings.
  • Delete travel records from within the app.

10. Security

TravelBug relies on Apple platform protections and secure transport (HTTPS) for supported network requests. No method of storage or transmission is guaranteed to be 100% secure.

11. Children’s Privacy

TravelBug is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

12. International Users

If you use third-party services (such as Google APIs, Apple services, or Aviationstack), your data may be processed in countries where those providers operate.

13. Policy Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Material updates will be reflected by revising the “Last Updated” date above.
